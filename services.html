Services

Precise, detail-oriented work — restored to new or near-new condition.

Deep cleaning and premium protection are at the core of every project. It’s hands-on work that takes real time to do right, and it restores your furniture for a fraction of what replacing it would cost.

SERVICE 01

Cleaning

The starting point for every project, and the foundation of the finished result. We hand-scrub away years of gray weathering, mildew, pollen, and grime — slow, careful, detail-oriented work, not a rushed pressure-washing pass that tears up the grain.

  • Hand-scrubbed, not pressure-blasted — safe for teak, wicker, and mixed-material sets
  • Removes mildew and surface staining down to the wood
  • Often paired with a fresh protector application for maintenance-only visits
SERVICE 02

Protecting

Once your furniture is clean, we apply premium protective coats — trusted products like Gold Care and Summer Classics, chosen based on what your specific piece needs. It seals in the finish and slows re-graying from Alabama’s sun, humidity, and pollen, so the work holds up season after season instead of fading by the following summer.

  • Premium, sun- and moisture-resistant protective products
  • Slows re-graying — extends time between deep cleans
  • Available as a standalone annual maintenance visit
AS NEEDED

Sanding

Most pieces only need a deep clean and a fresh protector application — sanding isn’t part of every job. But furniture that’s gone past that point — deeply grayed, rough, or cracking at the surface — may need it as an additional step. When it’s actually called for, we hand-sand back to bare, honey-toned wood, respecting the grain instead of grinding through it.

  • Added only when a piece’s condition actually requires it
  • Multi-stage hand sanding, done by feel — not a single pass
  • Restores the natural amber tone underneath the gray
Pricing

Fair pricing, built around your furniture — not a price sheet.

We don’t sell a flat per-chair rate off a laminated card. Every quote reflects the actual labor and materials your specific pieces need.

Why labor-based, scope-dependent pricing?

Two dining sets that look similar can need very different amounts of hands-on work — one might need a light clean and a protector refresh, another might need sanding first. Flat pricing either overcharges the simple job or undercuts the harder one, so we’d rather quote it honestly. This is careful, detail-oriented work, which means we’re not the cheapest option out there — but restoring your furniture to new or near-new condition still typically costs a fraction of replacing it outright.

Piece Count & Size

A two-seat bistro set and an 8-chair dining set take very different amounts of hands-on time.

Current Condition

Light graying vs. deep weathering, cracking, or mildew changes how much cleaning — and occasionally sanding — is required.

Scope of Service

Clean-only, or clean plus protector application — with sanding added when a piece actually needs it — each stage adds defined labor and material cost.
